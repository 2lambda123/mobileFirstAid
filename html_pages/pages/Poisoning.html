POISONING

Poison is any substance that is harmful to the body. People, especially children, poison themselves (deliberate or accidental) by eating or drinking wrong things or by taking too much medicine or tablets. There are several ways one can be exposed to poison. You could breathe it in, swallow it, or absorb it through your skin.

Poison on the Skin:

  Carefully remove any contaminated clothing

  Wash exposed areas with copious amounts of room temperature water.

  Take the person to the health centre immediately.

Poison in the Eyes:

  Rinse eyes with a slow gentle stream of water from a cup or a jug for 10 to 15 minutes.

  Allow the stream to flow from the inner corner across the eye to the outer corner.

  Do not apply eye drops.

  Take the person to the health centre immediately.

Swallowed Poison:

  If the person is alert, Do not induce vomiting.

  Immediately rinse the mouth.

  Keep the product or medicine container handy.

  Take the person to the health centre immediately.

Inhaled Poison:

  Do not make the person vomit.

  Immediately get the person to fresh air, without placing yourself at risk.

  Loosen any tight clothing at the neck.

  Open doors and windows, if indoors.

  Avoid breathing fumes.

  Do not try to rescue an unconscious person, where a highly toxic or unknown gas is involved, without a breathing apparatus. You may become a victim yourself.

  Take the person to the health centre immediately.

Swallowed Acids in batteries or Caustic soda:

  Make the person vomit by putting your finger in their throat or by making them drink water with salt.

  Take the person to the health centre immediately.

Swallowed Kerosene or Petrol:

  Do not make the person vomit.

  Give the person milk or water to drink in order to dilute the poison in the stomach.

  Take the person to the health centre immediately.
