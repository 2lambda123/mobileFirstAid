BLEEDING

Bleeding, also called hemorrhage, is the name used to describe blood loss. It can refer to blood loss as a result of damage blood vessels or organs

Get Help:

  Help the injured person lie down.

  Remove any clothing or debris on the wound but don't probe it unnecessarily.

  If the bleeding is small, wash it out with soap and water.

  Don’t remove an object if it’s embedded in the body.

  Stop the bleeding by placing a sterile bandage or clean cloth on the wound. Press the bandage firmly with your palm to control bleeding.

  Apply constant pressure until the bleeding stops.

  Take the victim to the health centre immediately.
