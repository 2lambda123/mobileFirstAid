BURNS

Burns are capable of causing damage to the skin or deeper tissues depending on the degree. Burns can be categorised as Major and Minor burns. Major burns are deep, cause the skin to be dry and leathery. Skin may appear charred or have patches of white, brown or black. Major burns are larger than 3 inches (about 8 centimeters) in diameter or cover the hands, feet, face,buttocks or a major joint.

For major burns

  Move the victim away from the source of the burn

  If the victim is not breathing perform Cardio Pulmonary Rescucitation if you can.

  Remove clothings and restrictive items from their body, such as belts and jewelry in or near the burned areas

  Don’t try to remove clothing that’s stuck to the skin.

  Cover the burned area with clean cloth or bandage that is moistened with cool, clean water.

  If hands and feet are burned, separate the fingers and toes with dry and sterile, nonadhesive bandages.

  Avoid immersing the person or burned body parts in water.

  Watch out for shock.

  Take the victim to the health centre immediately.

For minor burns:

  Cool down the burn under running water then apply cool, wet compresses until the pain subsides.

  Gently remove tight items, such as rings, from the burned area.

  Avoid breaking blisters.

  If a blister breaks, clean the area and gently apply an antibiotic ointment.

  Apply a moisturizing lotion to the affected part

  Loosely bandage the burn with a sterile gauze

  Take an over-the-counter pain reliever if necessary.

  Take the victim to the health centre immediately.
